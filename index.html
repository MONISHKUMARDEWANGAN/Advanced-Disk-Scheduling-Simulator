Algorithm Info
FCFS — Requests served in arrival order. Zero reordering. Simplest possible strategy but leads to the convoy effect — large zig-zag head movement across the disk.
SSTF — Greedy: always pick the nearest unserved cylinder. Minimises individual jumps but can cause starvation for far requests. This simulator implements request aging to prevent it.
SCAN — Elevator algorithm. Head sweeps one direction, serves all requests, hits disk end, then reverses. Fairer than SSTF. Base of Linux CFQ scheduler.
C-SCAN — Like SCAN but services only one direction. On reaching the end, jumps back to cylinder 0. Provides the most uniform wait time across all cylinders.
LOOK — Optimised SCAN. Head reverses at the last actual request, not at the disk boundary. Eliminates wasted travel to disk ends — more efficient in practice.
